## Problem
space_opera binds the **SWN (Stars Without Number)** ruleset. Its inventory is the worst
case in the 2026-06-14 cross-pack inventory audit (`docs/inventory-audit-2026-06-14.md`):

- The three worlds — `aureate_span`, `coyote_star`, `perseus_cloud` — each ship a
  `genre_packs/space_opera/worlds/<world>/inventory.yaml` that is **byte-identical**
  (md5 `80a030e3ba083b55f1eeae8b7e88fec1`, 278 lines each). 100% triplication, the worst
  duplication of any pack.
- **Zero** world-distinct gear: nothing differentiates the three settings mechanically.
- **Zero** `tech_level` / `tl` tags anywhere — SWN's tech-level model is entirely absent.
- Gear is hand-authored bespoke flavor with an SWN-*shaped* envelope bolted on, not sourced
  from the SWN equipment schema (the audit's universal finding).

## Technical Approach
_Grounding for the RED/design phase. The placement decision below is explicitly left to
TEA/Architect — SM is not prescribing it._

The story title has four clauses:
1. **De-triplicate** the three byte-identical world catalogs.
2. **Hoist the shared core** to a single source of truth.
3. **Add world-distinct gear** to each of the three worlds (reflecting its setting).
4. **Backfill SWN `tech_level` tags** on items.

**Load-bearing constraints:**
- **SWN licensing = DERIVE-ONLY, NOT verbatim.** SWN's free edition carries *no* open
  license (copyright only) — unlike the CC0 WWN packs and unlike sibling story **114-12**
  (CWN/WN *verbatim* rooting). The catalog must be **re-stated against the SWN schema, not
  copied** from the SRD. This is the single most important difference from the verbatim
  siblings — do not reproduce SWN tables verbatim.
- **WN-family item schema** (one schema covers all four Without-Number games):
  `{name, damage, attribute, encumbrance, cost, tech_level?, traits[]}`
  + ranged extras (`range`, `magazine`), + melee `shock`. SWN armor: `{ac, cost, enc, tl}`.
- **SOUL — Bind the Ruleset, Don't Balance It (content edition):** gear should *be* SWN gear
  (SWN schema, SWN-coherent stats), not bespoke gear tuned to look SWN. But **derive**, don't
  copy, for SWN.

**OPEN DESIGN QUESTION (TEA/Architect to resolve — do NOT assume):**
Where does the "shared core" live? ADR-140 says genre = rulebook/mechanics only and the
**world owns the catalog/cast** (gear has historically routed to world `items.yaml` at world
tier). De-triplication argues for a single shared baseline. How a shared SWN core coexists
with world-owned catalogs — a genre-tier SWN baseline that worlds layer over via ADR-121
per-field merge, vs. a shared include, vs. some other mechanism — is a design call for the
RED phase, not for SM. Whatever lands must keep ADR-140 placement honest and let each world
override/extend with bespoke gear.

## Scope
- **In scope:** the three `space_opera/worlds/{aureate_span,coyote_star,perseus_cloud}/inventory.yaml`
  catalogs — de-triplication, shared-core hoist, per-world distinct gear, SWN `tech_level` backfill,
  derived (not copied) SWN-schema stats.
- **Out of scope:** the `CatalogItem` model / engine changes (code side, route to Dev separately),
  the Fate-quartet ruleset work, other genres' inventories, and the SRD→inventory extraction tool.

## Acceptance Criteria
_Seeds — TEA refines/owns during RED._
- The three world `inventory.yaml` files are **no longer byte-identical**; the shared SWN core
  lives in exactly one place.
- Each of the three worlds carries **≥ a few world-distinct items** reflecting its setting.
- Items carry SWN **`tech_level`** tags (no longer zero).
- SWN gear is **derived against the SWN schema** — no verbatim SRD copy.
- Genre-pack validation passes (`sidequest-validate`); no broken artifact refs or orphan
  loadout keys introduced.
- ADR-140 placement remains honest (worlds can still own/override their catalog).
